Twitch and E-Sports

Toxic Twitch Streamers

Many Overwatch players enjoy watching professionals play the game. Esport tournaments are spread out and infrequent so many people turn to Twitch. Twitch is a streaming platform where you can watch others play games live and Overwatch has a big following on Twitch. You can look at how many people are watching Overwatch streams right now by clicking this link. The issue is that some of the most popular streamers are bad role models. While there a lot of different kinds of streamers, many yell at their teammates when losing and transfer blame away from themselves. This teaches impressionable younger players that is how they should treat people in the game. This mentality is what breads toxicity at all levels.

Can You Learn From Pro Players in Overwatch?

The short answer to this question is no you can not. Pro players are the equivalent of pro basketball players in that their skill is on another planet. The mechanical movement skills and aiming is one that take decades to perfect and there reaction time is superhuman. The only way to improve these skills is by playing not by watching.

Another reason you can’t replicate the strategies of a pro team with that of your team of strangers is the idea of communication. The level of communication dependent to play this game well is insane and pro teams practice 6 hours a day at least together to develop that level of expertise with each other. Often people will try to organize team compositions and strategies like the pros and when people inevitably don’t listen or perform their task wrong people get angry. These task masters will become very toxic when they don’t play the way the US National team plays. This is yet another way that the pros contribute indirectly to toxicity.
