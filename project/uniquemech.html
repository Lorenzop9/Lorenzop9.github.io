The Transition from Conventional First Person Shooter

Overwatch is a first person shooter but it is very different than the traditional call of duty game. Most first person shooters involve you picking up a gun, as an animated human, and simulating actual gunfights. People die quickly and the guns look the ones the army uses today. Overwatch has characters who are animals, hold almost magical abilities, and fight in ways that are nothing like war. Coming from a game like CoD or Halo the skills are not as transferable as you would believe. This makes the pro Halo player pissed off when they first start playing because they have to start from the beginning. Unique Movement

All of these characters also have very different movement speeds. In other first person shooters there are forms of futuristic movement. Double jumping, wall running, flying briefly, etc. This game is different because each character has different movement.

Genji has increased speed walking and can double jump. Phara can fly for set periods of time. DVA (a tank) can also fly for a time much shorter than Phara. Hanzo (an archer) can climb walls. And most significantly Lucio (shown above) can actually ride on walls and speed boost. With every character having their own movement there are spots on each map that only a portion of characters can reach, or places that are difficult to reach for some and easy for others. This creates an interesting dynamic for newcomers who do not know how to navigate the tricky maps. All of these differences to the traditional FPS is why Overwatch players can become so toxic.
