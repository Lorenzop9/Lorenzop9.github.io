The rating system that leaves everyone thinking they are misplaced

How Hard is it to Climb The Ranks?

A typical game in Overwatch lasts between 10-20 minutes. In the competitive latter you must increase by 500 points to go up a rank. For example going 2100- 2600 will increase your rank from gold to platinum. On each win you will win somewhere between 15-35 points and a loss will tank you score 15-35 points. This variability will depend on your performance in the game. If you do very well on the winning team you can win points in the 30 and if you are on the losing team you will lose less than 20 and inversely if you do poorly you will gain less and lose more. So to climb a rank you must win 20 more games than you lose and thats 5 hours of straight winning. So the answer is it is really hard.

What is The Competitive System in Overwatch?

The competitive system in Overwatch makes up the bulk of time played on the game. You can play other game modes that do not affect rating but most players will play competitive. You will get a rating 1-5000 sr or season rank. The system gives different rankings based on your sr shown below.

The lowest most people get on this scale is around 600 and the highest is around 4700. The top 500 players in Grand Master will get the ranking of top 500 where they will actually compete for the number one spot of the highest sr. A season in Overwatch lasts 60 day and players have that long to get the highest score they can. When the season resets there is a 3 day off-season. When a season restarts you must complete 10 placement matches so the system can put you into the correct ran.
